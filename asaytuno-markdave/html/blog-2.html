Commit Your Plans (Even Your Code)

Mark Dave • Feb 22, 2026 • 3 min read

I don’t commit code every day. Sometimes I’m stuck debugging. Sometimes I feel unmotivated. But whenever I do commit, it means one thing, it means progress.

In programming, if you don’t commit, your work isn’t saved. You can spend hours coding, but without committing, it can disappear. It’s like it never happened. Life feels the same.

We all have plans to finish our degree, to make our family proud, to build something meaningful. But sometimes we just think about them. We don’t fully commit.

“Commit to the Lord whatever you do, and He will establish your plans.” — Proverbs 16:3

As a student with big dreams, I’ve learned that commitment isn’t about being perfect. It’s about deciding that your dream matters. Just like in coding, I can’t control every error. But I can commit. I can take small steps. I can trust the process.

Maybe I don’t commit code every day. But I want to commit my plans every day. Because uncommitted dreams stay unfinished.

— Mark Dave
